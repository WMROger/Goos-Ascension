Water prefabs vs grid tilemap water
====================================

Why grid "Tilemap_Water" works but a dragged prefab might not:

1) Tag + layer
   - PlayerMovement treats volumes as water if Tag is "Water" OR layer is "Water".
   - Tilemap_Water in scenes is usually set to Tag: Water + Water layer.
   - These prefabs use Tag: Water and the Water layer — match your grid setup.

2) Trigger volume must overlap the player
   - Tilemap water uses TilemapCollider2D over every painted cell = large overlap.
   - WaterReflection / WaterSimple use a BoxCollider2D (~1 unit tall in local space).
     Move/scale the object so the GREEN collider gizmo actually covers the player.

3) Default transform
   - Prefab root is saved at (0,0,-1) so new instances spawn at the scene origin.
     Position the whole object where your pool is (tilemap water stays under Grid at 0,0).

4) BoxCollider offset
   - WaterReflection has a wide BoxCollider offset to match the generated mesh width.
     Collider is centered on the mesh; move the whole GameObject, not only the sprite.

5) Buoyancy Effector
   - Collider Mask often excludes Player; slime/human behavior is mostly from PlayerMovement.
   - If you add Player to the mask, both forms get buoyancy from the effector.
